Design Process

Step 1: Researching a Design Domain

We began our research in the domain of international students. We conducted interviews on six international students who attend a post-secondary school in Vancouver. We asekd respondents questions regarding:

  • Their personal background and field of study
  • Their concerns about school life, both in and out of school
  • Tools or apps they use, and their frustrations with them
  • Methods of socializing and connecting within the student community
  • Meaningful advice they've received throughout their school life
  • Their strategies for searching for jobs, internships, or co-op positions
  • Their frustrations with administrative processes in academia

Our research revealed that respondents faced challenges finding accessible and affordable housing in Vancouver due to rising prices and high demand from international students, resulting in limited options within students' price range.

Step 2: Ideation and Interaction Wireflows

Based on our research, we developed an idea for a roommate and housing finder app that would allow students to match with appropriate housing and compatible roommates. We then defined the main flows of the app and created grey-box wireflows for them.

Step 3: Interactive Prototype and Style Guide

In this stage, we began developing a visual brand for our app. We created a style guide and mock-ups for our interface, in addition to creating an interactive prototype.

Step 4: User Testing

After developing our interactive prototype, we began user testing on six potential primary-users of our app. The feedback we received were an equal mix of both user-experience and efficiency issues.

  • Some buttons and UI elements are not obvious that they can be clicked.
  • Besides filtering listings by recency and price, it would be nice if we could filter by roommate preferences within the listing results as well.
  • For privacy purposes, it may be enough to have users list only their first name.
  • Some buttons are not working in the demo.
  • There is no “previous” option when choosing my ideal roommate.
  • The search bar is not very practical, and the move-in date for renting is a bit strange.
  • Most houses are rented starting from a specific month, not a specific date.
  • Text is too small and not legible on some pages.
